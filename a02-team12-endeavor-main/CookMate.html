Save and Follow Your Favorite Recipes

  • Create or log in to your CookMate account.
  • Find the recipe you'd like to save or follow.
  • Tap the heart icon to add the recipe to your favorites.
  • Press the "Follow" button to explore the recipe in detail.
Create and Add Your Own Recipes

  • Go to the "Home" section of the app.
  • Select the "Add Recipe" button located at the bottom right.
  • Complete the necessary fields, including recipe title, ingredients, and steps.
  • Save the recipe to your personal collection.
Get Detailed Recipe Information, Including Servings, Prep Time, and More

  • Select a recipe from the app's home screen or search results.
  • View the detailed recipe page, by clicking on "follow" button.
  • Discover the magic numbers: servings, prep time, cook time, ingredients etc...
Q & A

  • Q1: What would you do differently, if you had the chance to start over?

    • If we had the chance to start over, we would make a few changes to our approach. Firstly, we would start earlier in each iteration to allow for better planning and to have more time to deal with adjustments and changes. Additionally, we would set more small checkpoints in each iteration to monitor our progress and ensure we were on track to meet our goals.

  • Q2: How did the project change from your initial (iteration 0) vision or stories, or did it work out as predicted?

    • Our project underwent some changes from our initial vision in iteration 0. While we had originally planned to include more user stories, such as recipe categories and filters, some of these features didn't work out and were discarded. However, we were still able to deliver a high-quality product that met our original expectations, with the exception of the unfinished user stories.

  • Q3: What did you learn about team or large project development? What will you start doing, keep doing, or stop doing next time?

    • We learned that facilitating communication between team members is crucial for the development process to run smoothly. We did our best to organize meetings in between iterations in order to set expectations, divide work, and ensure that we were all on the same page; although we worked fairly independently, we were able to develop a cohesive and functional final product. We also learned the importance of proper documentation and commenting on code. Next time, commenting will be a priority so all team members can quickly and easily understand all portions of the code.
